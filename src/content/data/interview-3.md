# 🔌 Texas Instruments Analog Design Engineer Experience

This is my interview experience for the Analog Design Engineer profile at TI.

## 📝 Round 1: Written Test (NITC Campus)
The test had section-wise cutoffs:
*   **Aptitude:** Basic math, logical reasoning, and verbal.
*   **Core Technical:** Questions on Op-Amps, RC circuits, transfer functions, and basic BJT/MOSFET characteristics. Very technical and time-constrained.

## 👥 Round 2: Technical Interview (Core Analog)
This round lasted for nearly an hour and a half. The interviewers asked me to draw circuits and derive equations:
*   **Topic 1:** Bode plots for multi-pole systems. Draw and explain phase margins.
*   **Topic 2:** Op-Amp non-idealities (offset voltage, slew rate, input bias current). How to compensate offset voltage in a differential amplifier?
*   **Topic 3:** Derive the voltage gain and input impedance of a Cascode Amplifier stage.

## 🛠️ Round 3: Project Discussion & HR
Deep dive into my final year project on "High-Efficiency DC-DC Boost Converter". They asked specific questions on magnetics design and inductor saturation limits.
This was followed by basic HR questions regarding relocation and team preferences.

*Verdict: Selected!*
