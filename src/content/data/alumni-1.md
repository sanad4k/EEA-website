### Q: How did you decide between a Job vs. a Master's immediately after B.Tech?
I actually worked for one year at a consultancy firm in Bangalore before applying for my Master's.

* **Why I chose a Master's:** I realized that to get into R&D roles in core companies (like Tesla, Apple, or Rimac), a specialized depth of knowledge is often required. Undergraduate courses cover the breadth, but a Master's gave me the specific skills in *Electrochemical Storage* and *Control Theory* that I use daily.
* **Advice:** If you are 100% sure about your niche, you can go directly. If you are unsure, working for 1-2 years gives you clarity and makes your SOP (Statement of Purpose) much stronger.

### Q: What colleges/courses should EEE students target for EV roles?
For Electric Vehicles, you want programs that blend **Power Electronics**, **Control Systems**, and **Embedded Systems**.
* **Top US Schools:** Stanford, Georgia Tech, Purdue, and UM Ann Arbor (strong automotive ties).
* **Top European Schools:** TU Delft (Netherlands) and RWTH Aachen (Germany) are excellent and often more affordable.

### Q: What does a "Battery Engineer" actually do day-to-day?
It's not just sitting at a desk! My week is split 60/40 between simulation and the lab.
* **Simulation:** Using **MATLAB/Simulink** to model how the battery behaves under extreme heat or cold.
* **Testing:** We physically crash, heat, and age battery cells to validate our models.
* **Cross-functional work:** I talk to the Mechanical team (cooling pipes) and the Firmware team (coding the BMS) constantly.

### Q: What are the specific technical skills Tesla looks for?

### Q: What should I focus on in my 3rd and 4th year?
* **Don't ignore the labs:** The Machines Lab and Power Electronics Lab are old-school, but the fundamentals you learn there (like torque-speed characteristics) are asked in interviews.
* **Build a Portfolio:** Don't just list projects on a resume. Create a GitHub repo or a simple website documenting *how* you built it, the failure points, and the final result.
* **Network:** Reach out to alumni on LinkedIn. Don't ask "Can you give me a referral?" straight away. Ask specific questions about their work. We are usually happy to help!

> **Final thought:** The EV industry is booming. Don't be afraid to aim for "Dream Companies" straight out of Kerala. The foundation we get at NITC is solid enough to compete globally.
